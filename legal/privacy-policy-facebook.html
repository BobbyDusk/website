Privacy Policy

Effective Date: June 21, 2026

This Privacy Policy explains how our Facebook Integration App for Postiz ("the App") processes, secures, and handles your data when you link your Facebook assets via the Meta for Developers Platform.

1. Information We Access and Collect

To enable cross-posting and analytics features within Postiz, this App accesses information through official Meta Graph API OAuth permissions granted by you:

  • User Profile Info & Permissions: Basic profile details to authorize ownership of associated Pages.
  • Page/Group Metadata: Names, profile images, and unique IDs of the Facebook Pages or Groups you manage, to display them as target channels inside Postiz.
  • Access Tokens: Secure short-lived and long-lived Page access tokens required to perform API requests on your behalf.
  • Content Media: Images, videos, links, and post text data scheduled within your Postiz instance for delivery to Meta.

2. How Data is Processed

Data accessed via Meta APIs is strictly used to fulfill your manual or scheduled publishing commands. If you are self-hosting Postiz, all access tokens and post logs are stored locally on your own infrastructure. We do not operate central servers that log or store your Meta access history.

3. Data Sharing & Third Parties

We do not share, sell, or rent any personal or account data to third parties. Data transfers occur strictly between your Postiz installation and Meta's official Graph API endpoints via TLS encryption.

4. Data Retention and Deletion

Data and active access tokens persist within your Postiz deployment environment for as long as your channel connection is active. You can purge this data entirely at any time.

5. User Rights and Access Revocation

You maintain absolute ownership and control over your data permissions:

  • Inside Postiz: Deleting the Facebook channel from your Postiz instance drops and wipes the relevant tokens immediately.
  • Inside Facebook: You can completely revoke this App’s access privileges at any time by going to your Facebook Account Settings > Apps and Websites and removing this integration.

6. Data Deletion Callback (Meta Compliance)

In accordance with Meta Developer Policies, if you wish to formally request data deletion or execute a data deletion callback, removing the integration via Facebook's "Apps and Websites" menu will trigger Meta's native de-authorization flow, which immediately deprecates all active Graph API tokens held within your local client instance.

7. Contact Information

For data governance questions regarding your local Postiz installation, please contact your environment administrator.
